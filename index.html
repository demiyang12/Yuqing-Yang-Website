03 / About

About Me

I'm a spatial analyst and urban planner pursuing my Master of Urban Spatial Analytics (MUSA) at the University of Pennsylvania's Weitzman School of Design (GPA 3.9/4.0), expected May 2026. Before Penn, I earned my Bachelor of Engineering in Urban and Rural Planning from Shenzhen University in 2025.

Cities are the most complex datasets ever built — I want to help decode them.

My work bridges quantitative analysis and spatial storytelling. I'm especially interested in traffic safety, housing equity, and transit accessibility — and building tools that make these issues legible to planners and policymakers.

I've collaborated with the City of Philadelphia's Office of Transportation and Infrastructure Systems (OTIS) on Vision Zero research, interned as an Assistant Urban Analyst at Shenzhen's Geospatial Information Center, and published research at the 2024 Annual National Planning Conference.
