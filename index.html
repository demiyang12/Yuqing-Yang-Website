03 / About

About Me

How I See Cities
Cities are the most complex datasets ever built — I want to help decode them.

I believe the city is fundamentally a spatial problem — one where transport networks, housing markets, land use, and human behavior interact across scales. My work lives at the intersection of rigorous spatial analysis and clear communication: turning complex data into tools that planners and policymakers can actually use.

What I Do

I'm especially drawn to traffic safety, housing equity, and transit accessibility — problems where data can directly inform more just and efficient cities. I build predictive models, interactive maps, and decision-support tools. I don't just analyze; I build systems that help others ask better questions.

I've collaborated with the City of Philadelphia's Office of Transportation and Infrastructure Systems (OTIS) on Vision Zero research, and published research at the 2024 Annual National Planning Conference.

Where I Come From

I grew up in China and trained as an urban planner at Shenzhen University (Bachelor of Engineering in Urban and Rural Planning, 2025). Shenzhen — a city that grew from fishing village to megacity in 40 years — shaped how I think about rapid urbanization and the gap between planning vision and lived reality.

Now at Penn's Weitzman School of Design pursuing my Master of Urban Spatial Analytics (MUSA) (GPA 3.9/4.0, expected May 2026), I've gained fluency in both the computational methods and the American planning context. I also interned as an Assistant Urban Analyst at Shenzhen's Geospatial Information Center.
